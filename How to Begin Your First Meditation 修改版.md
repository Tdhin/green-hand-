# How to Begin Your First Meditation
## Audience
- you've never meditated before
- you are too busy to do research on meditation
- you are interested in meditation but don't know how to begin
## What This Guide Covers
- A 5-10 minute step-by-step meditation you can do it now 
- Bottom line of meditation 
- details about whether you are doing meditation rightly
## What This Guide Doesn't Cover
- Different meditation traditions or beliefs
- How to meditate for anxiety , a good sleep or for other specific goals
- Advanced techniques or long-duration meditation
## What Meditation Actually Is
Meditation is essentially allowance,not to chase, control or to struggle.A real meditation is to see what is happening in the stand of the third one, not to ananlyze ,judge or correct yourself.
## How to Meditate
### Step 1: Choose a meditation technique
There are more than 52 meditation techniques, and they can be classified into 3 varieties,icluding:
- scanning the body 
- observing the abdomen while breathing 
- observing thoughts
  
The **<ins>second one** is the most popular technique,so it is what we focus on to practice here.
### Step 2: Find a comfortable and quiet space
 Choose a spot where others will rarely disturb you.
- a corner of your bed room or a comfortable chair
- quiet outdoors, like in forrest or a workplace (if you need to, tell people you need a few uninterrupted minutes )
- Prioritize comfort and good posture. A bed or cushion works fine.(elements like a small plant, soft lighting or incense is optional )
### Step 3: Set a  timer for 5 to 10 minutes
- Start with a shorter time, 5 to 10 minutes , is better for a sustainable practice. Even with short sessions, when done regularly, your meditation can provide benefits.
- You can use phone alarm clock or a professional meditation app,some of which won't stop playing soft music even when they go off
### Step 4:Position your body
what matters most is to choose a position where you feel comfortable but not too relaxed.
- **Seated in a chair** : Seat in the front edge of your chair with your feet flat on the floor.Keep your back straight but not rigid, allowing for a natural curve of your spine.
- **Cross-legged on a cushion** : sit on a firm cushion or a folded blanket with your legs crossed in front of you.
- **Kneeling position** : kneel on your meditation bench or cushion, sitting back on your heels.   
whichever position you choose, try to keep your head balanced naturally over your spine and rest your hands on your laps or thighs.Close your eyes gently or keep them slightly open with a soft gaze directed at the floor a few feet in front of you.        
### Step 5：Bring your awareness to your breath and your body.
- take several deeper breaths to settle yourself.
- direct your attention to the physical sensation of breathing.
- feel the expansion and contraction of your abdomen.
### Step 6: Count when you breath
- belly breathing: with your inhaling, feel your abdomen rising,and with your exhaling, feel your abdomen dropping slowly
- After you master the abdominal respiration, you can try count up to 5 when you inhale and do the same when you exhale.
- If you lose track of your count or notice your mind has wandered, just start again without judging yourself.
### Step 7: Ending movement
- Warm your hands up and then put them on your eyes
- After you open your eyes,slowly move your hands and legs
- Drink a cup of warm water or electrolyte water to keep your body fluid balanced.
## Problems That May Appear During Meditation And Corresponding Solutions
Problem 1:
 
 What should you do if you feel a strong need to move (perhaps adjust an uncomfortable position or respond to an itch during meditation?)

Solutions 1:
 
 try first observing the sensation with curiosity.Often, the urge will pass. If the discomfort continues, make any necessary adjustment mindfully and with intention, then return your body to stillness.

Problem 2:

What should you do if your mind wanders during meditation?

Solution 2:
- Acknowledge the thought without judgment
- Gently let the thought go, like releasing a balloon into the sky
- Return your attention to your breath and counting

Problem 3: 

What should you do when you find it difficult for you to meditate regularly?

Solution 3:
- As a beginner, aim for achievable daily practice goals of just 5 to 10 minutes
- Consider meditating at the same time each day to build a habit—such as first thing in the morning or right before bed.
- Add a reminder to your phone or calendar to help you remember and track your progress to stay motivated.
##What To Expect
You may find one or all of following changes in yourself after several meditations:

- You will improve your concentration, accordingly you will do your work efficiency and ability to focus on what people say when you are interacting with them.
- Release your anxiety and pressure if there exist.
- Strengthen your immunity and bring you back to a healthy walk of life.
